What Is Retatrutide?

Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a synthetic peptide developed by Eli Lilly. It is classified as a triple agonist, targeting three receptors simultaneously: the glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor (GLP-1R), the glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide receptor (GIPR), and the glucagon receptor (GCGR). This triple mechanism distinguishes it from earlier generation compounds such as semaglutide (GLP-1 single agonist) and tirzepatide (GLP-1/GIP dual agonist).

The compound's engagement of the glucagon receptor is of particular scientific interest. Glucagon receptor agonism increases hepatic glucose output and thermogenesis, and researchers have proposed this as the mechanism responsible for the enhanced weight loss signals observed relative to dual agonists. The Phase 2 data published in 2023 reported mean body weight reductions of up to 24.2% over 48 weeks at the highest dose studied, a figure that attracted significant attention across metabolic research.

Reconstitution and Handling

Retatrutide is supplied as a lyophilised white powder in sealed vials. For laboratory use, it is typically reconstituted using bacteriostatic water or sterile saline. The standard reconstitution approach involves adding the solvent slowly down the side of the vial to avoid mechanical disruption to the peptide structure. Solutions should be swirled gently rather than shaken.

Once reconstituted, solutions should be stored at 4 degrees Celsius and used within 28 days. Freeze-thaw cycles degrade the reconstituted compound and should be avoided. For long-term storage of the lyophilised powder, minus 20 degrees Celsius is recommended.
